blank collar

/blæŋk ˈkɒl.ə/ noun

  1. 1.

    A worker or operator whose role transcends the traditional blue-collar and white-collar divide; someone running an AI-native business where automation handles routine work and humans focus on judgment, taste, and outcomes.

    Example “She left her white-collar consulting job to become a blank collar founder, running her entire operation through a small team and a fleet of AI agents.”

  2. 2.

    The emerging class of operators in the post-automation economy — defined not by uniform or industry, but by leverage.

    Example “The blank collar economy doesn’t care where you work, only what you ship.”

Etymology

From blank (“empty, undefined, open”) + collar, after blue-collar and white-collar. The qualifier “blank” denotes liberation from rigid classification — work defined by outcomes rather than attire, location, or trade.
